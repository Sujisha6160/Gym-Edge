Hammer Curl

Other Classes

The Hammer Curl is a bicep exercise that also targets the brachialis (a muscle beneath the biceps) and the brachioradialis in the forearms, making it excellent for building overall arm size and strength. It’s performed with dumbbells held in a neutral grip, meaning your palms face each other throughout the movement, similar to holding a hammer — hence the name. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, keeping your core engaged and shoulders relaxed. Start with the dumbbells at your sides, elbows tucked close to your torso, and curl the weights upward in a controlled motion without swinging your arms or using momentum. At the top of the movement, squeeze your biceps for a brief pause, then slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position to maximize muscle engagement. Breathing is important — exhale while lifting and inhale while lowering. The hammer curl not only builds thicker biceps but also improves forearm strength, which benefits grip and overall pulling power in other exercises. Beginners should focus on lighter weights with perfect form before progressing to heavier loads to avoid straining the wrists or elbows. For variety, hammer curls can be done seated, alternated between arms, or using cables or resistance bands to keep constant tension on the muscles. Consistently adding them to your arm workouts will help create a more well-rounded and powerful upper body appearance.
